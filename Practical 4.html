Here are the key differences between lists and tables: 1. Purpose Lists: Lists are used to present a collection of items or points in a sequential or non-sequential manner. They are ideal for displaying items that do not require a structured format, such as bullet points, steps in a process, or a series of related items. Tables: Tables are used to present data in a structured format, typically in rows and columns. They are ideal for displaying related data that can be compared, such as statistics, schedules, or any dataset that requires organization into categories. 2. Structure Lists: Lists can be ordered (
